# RFC-002: Embedded Integration Mode (TMF-Insertion)

## TMF as a Physics-Grounded Safety Supervisory Layer

**Status:** Draft / Request for Comments
**Author:** Mingrong Zhao (with AI engineering collaborators)
**License:** AGPL-3.0
**Target Audience:** Autonomous Driving Architects, Functional Safety Engineers, Robotics Researchers

---

# 1. Objective

This RFC defines the TMF-Insertion architecture: a gradual embedded integration strategy that allows TMF to operate as a physics-grounded supervisory safety layer within existing autonomous systems.

Rather than replacing production driving stacks, TMF is designed to incrementally improve:

* physical interpretability,
* safety auditability,
* disagreement detection,
* and physics-level constraint supervision.

The integration philosophy prioritizes:

* minimal intrusion,
* measurable trust accumulation,
* and safe phased deployment.

---

# 2. Core Philosophy

TMF is not intended to immediately replace existing AI navigation systems.

Instead, TMF acts as an independent physical safety observer capable of:

* generating binary physical feasibility masks,
* detecting geometric inconsistencies,
* monitoring system disagreement,
* and gradually providing safety-oriented supervisory authority.

This architecture allows black-box systems to become more inspectable and physically auditable over time without requiring a full-stack redesign.

---

# 3. Integration Phases

## Phase 1 — Shadow Mode (Strongly Recommended Initial Deployment)

TMF operates fully in parallel with the production autonomy stack.

Capabilities:

* Listen-only operation
* Real-time physical mask generation
* Decision comparison against existing planner
* Logging of disagreement events
* Safety scoring and audit report generation

Restrictions:

* No control authority
* No planner interference
* No actuator access

Purpose:

* Validate long-term consistency
* Measure disagreement statistics
* Build trust through observational evidence

---

## Phase 2 — Warning Mode

TMF may issue warnings when severe physical inconsistencies or non-traversable conditions are detected.

Examples:

* unsafe slope estimation,
* medium discontinuity anomalies,
* impossible traversability conditions.

Authority:

* Warning only
* Human driver or supervisory system retains full control authority

Purpose:

* Introduce human-visible safety supervision
* Validate real-world usefulness of TMF alerts

---

## Phase 3 — Limited Physical Safety Override

In carefully constrained low-speed or low-risk scenarios, TMF may provide limited physical-layer intervention.

Example scenarios:

* parking maneuvers,
* emergency stop assistance,
* low-speed obstacle avoidance,
* impossible traversability prevention.

Constraints:

* Strict operational design domain (ODD)
* Low-speed only
* Limited-duration intervention

Purpose:

* Gradually evaluate closed-loop supervisory capability
* Reduce deployment and certification risk

---

## Phase 4 — Final Physical Safety Override

TMF may provide final physical-layer safety veto authority under predefined certified conditions.

Example:

If the physical feasibility mask determines:

* traversability = impossible,
* severe medium inconsistency,
* or unrecoverable safety conflict,

TMF may force transition into a predefined safe state.

Examples of safe-state behavior:

* controlled stop,
* minimal-risk maneuver,
* fallback handover.

Important Limitation:

Semantic planning authority remains external to TMF.

TMF supervises physical feasibility only.

---

# 4. Safety Fallback Principles

TMF must always support graceful fallback behavior.

If:

* confidence drops below threshold,
* sensor consistency collapses,
* temporal instability persists,
* or medium estimation becomes unreliable,

full authority automatically returns to the primary navigation system.

TMF must never assume authority outside its validated operational domain.

---

# 5. Engineering Motivation

The phased insertion strategy is designed to reduce industrial adoption barriers.

Benefits include:

* compatibility with existing autonomy stacks,
* incremental validation,
* lower certification burden,
* improved interpretability,
* and measurable disagreement auditing.

This approach enables gradual transition from:

passive observation → safety supervision → constrained physical intervention.
